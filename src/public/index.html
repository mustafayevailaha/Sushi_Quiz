What is Sushi?

Sushi (å¯¿å¸) is a traditional Japanese dish combining vinegared rice with seafood, vegetables, and occasionally tropical fruits. The word "sushi" refers to the specially prepared rice, not the fish!

History of Sushi

Sushi's origins date back to Southeast Asia around 2,000 years ago. Originally, it was a method of preserving fish in fermented rice.

Modern sushi as we know it today was developed in Edo (now Tokyo) in the early 19th century. Hanaya Yohei created "nigiri sushi" - hand-pressed sushi with fresh fish on vinegared rice.
